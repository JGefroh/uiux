Consider Form Layout

Forms should be easy to scan and fill out. That's why I recommend single-column forms.

Example (Bad)
We'll use this name in any emails we send you.
This is your department's 9-digit identifier.
Your employee ID (###-###) is used to identify you to all of our internal services.

This double column layout might save vertical space, but it makes everything else more difficult.

Your eye goes back and forth between labels and text fields. There's too much eye movement amongst the clutter.

You can't tell at a glance what the next field will be if you tab - will it go down or to the right?

You also limit the amount of space you have for validation/error messages, as well as format hints and other helpful context-building messages.

Example (Good)

We'll use this name in any emails we send you.

This is your department's 9-digit identifier.

Your Employee ID is required!

Your employee ID (###-###) is used to identify you to all of our internal services.

This single column layout takes more vertical space. However, it has enough room to display field validation messages as well as context-building hints.

While you fill out the form, your eye moves in a single direction - downward. If you ever need a hint, you can look right next to the field.
