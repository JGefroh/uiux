Avoid Only Icons

Using pure icons as buttons should be avoided. You don't know how your user is going to interpret the symbol or its surrounding context.

Example (Bad)
Does this mean Search? Inspect? Zoom? Identify? Everyone will have a different answer depending on their background.

Context matters. This may mean 'Edit' in a record program, 'Draw' in an art application, or 'Sign' in a document reader.
Example (Good)
Users don't have to think to figure this button out. The icon helps enhance the meaning.

Unless context is established effectively, it is better to be explicit.
