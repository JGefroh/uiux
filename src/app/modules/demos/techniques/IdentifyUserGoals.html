Identify Your Users' Goals

Users are going to be using your software to perform a task. Find out what that task is.

Let's say that you have a budget application that tracks expenditures and revenue.

If 95% of your users are going to be focused on entering data into this system, then you should focus on making the data-input process as painless as possible. On the other hand, if 95% of your users are managers that will be reading and interpreting financial reports and data, you would likely focus on providing intuitive dashboards and summary reports.

This must also be balanced with the principle of working on things that provide the most value first.
